# Step 11 - Explaining the Quote (Grandma Version)

---

## What are we actually doing here?

We don't just give a number and walk away. We explain WHY the quote is what it is. There are three different types of explanations, because there are three different things to explain.

---

## Explanation 1: Why does machining take this long? (SHAP)

The AI tells us which features of the part made machining time go up or down compared to an average part.

```
Average part takes 4.2 minutes
+ Grinding needed:  +1.8 min (biggest cost driver)
+ 6 deep holes:     +2.1 min
- Easy material:    -0.9 min (aluminium is softer)
+ Fine tolerance:   +0.3 min
= This part:        7.5 minutes
```

It's like a doctor explaining: "Your blood pressure is high because of your salt intake (+), but your exercise routine is helping (-)."

Important note: these “+/-” numbers are **minutes**, not rupees. The system turns minutes into rupees later by multiplying by the selected machine’s hourly rate.

Also: choosing a different machine changes the **rupee** cost, but it does not change what the AI predicted for time (the AI doesn’t take “which machine you picked” as an input).

---

## Explanation 2: How was each cost calculated? (Formulas)

Shows the exact math for everything that ISN'T AI:

```
Material: 1.6 kg x ₹85/kg x 0.9 discount = ₹122
Setup: ₹2000 / sqrt(50 units) = ₹283
Overhead: ₹530 x 15% = ₹80
```

No secrets - you can check every calculation with a calculator.

---

## Explanation 3: Why were those machines selected? (Routing)

The system first auto-detects which processes are needed (from the part geometry and requirements), and suggests machine options. The engineer can change the processes and/or machines in the dashboard.

If anything is changed manually, we label it as an **Engineer override**, and the quote uses the **final confirmed** routing and machine selections.

```
Auto (suggested):
Turning:  OFF - Part isn't elongated (aspect ratio below 2.0)
Milling:  ON  - 2 pockets detected
Drilling: ON  - 6 holes detected
Grinding: ON  - Fine tolerance requires finishing

Final (confirmed):
Milling machine:   VMC (selected)
Drilling machine:  Radial drill (selected)
Grinding machine:  Surface grinder (selected)
Engineer override: Yes (changed machine selection)
```

---

## Why three separate explanations?

Because mixing them up causes confusion:

- "Why is machining expensive?" -> Answer from SHAP (AI explanation)
- "How was setup cost calculated?" -> Answer from formulas
- "Why does it need grinding?" -> Answer from routing rules

Different questions need different types of answers.

---

## What does this step produce?

Three explanation panels that appear below the quote, so the engineer can understand and trust the numbers.
