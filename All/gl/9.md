# Step 9 - How Confident is the AI? (Grandma Version)

---

## What are we actually doing here?

The AI gave us a single number (say, 7.5 minutes). But how confident is it? Is it "definitely 7.5" or "somewhere between 5 and 10"?

This step figures out the confidence range - like a weather forecast saying "70-80% chance of rain" instead of just "it will rain."

---

## How does it figure out the range?

Remember, a Random Forest is actually 300 small AIs (decision trees) that each make their own guess. Normally we just average all 300 guesses. But here, we also look at how much the 300 guesses disagree.

- If all 300 guess between 7.0 and 8.0 -> very confident (narrow range)
- If guesses range from 4.0 to 12.0 -> not confident (wide range)

Important detail: this range is not a “math proof” confidence interval. It’s a practical uncertainty estimate based on how much the 300 trees disagree.

---

## Example

```
300 individual guesses: 6.1, 6.3, 7.4, 8.0, 8.8, ...

Average: 7.5 minutes
Low end (10th percentile): 6.2 minutes
High end (90th percentile): 8.9 minutes
```

So the system says: "Machining will take about 7.5 minutes, but could range from 6.2 to 8.9 minutes."

To make it easier to read, the system can also show a simple risk label:

- **Low** uncertainty (narrow range)
- **Medium** uncertainty
- **High** uncertainty (wide range)

---

## Why only machining has uncertainty

The other costs (material, setup, overhead) are calculated with simple formulas - there's no guessing involved. You multiply weight times price and get an exact number.

Only the machining time is predicted by AI, so only that part has uncertainty. The final cost range reflects just the machining uncertainty, while other costs stay fixed (the machining rupee range is computed using the selected machine rate(s)).

---

## What does this step produce?

A range (low to high) for machining time. In Step 10, we convert that time range into a rupee range using the final, user-selected machine(s) and their hourly rates.
