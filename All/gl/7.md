# Step 7 - Putting All the Puzzle Pieces Together (Grandma Version)

---

## What are we actually doing here?

We've collected information from three different sources:
- The computer measured the shape (Step 2)
- The engineer told us about holes, material, etc. (Step 3)
- The system suggested which processes are needed (Step 4) and showed machine options, and the engineer can adjust them

Now we combine everything into one neat list of 17 numbers. This list is what the AI reads to make its prediction.

Think of it like filling out a medical form before seeing the doctor - age, weight, blood pressure, symptoms - all in one place so the doctor can make a diagnosis.

---

## What's in the list?

**From the 3D shape:**
- Surface area (how much outside surface)
- Volume (how much stuff inside)
- Aspect ratio (long vs fat)
- Surface-to-volume ratio (thin-walled vs solid)
- Slenderness (how stick-like)
- “Pocket-heaviness” proxy (roughly how many pockets)

**From the engineer:**
- Number of holes
- Number of pockets
- Maximum depth
- Material type (saved as a category code, e.g., Aluminium=0, Steel=1, etc.)
- Tolerance level (Standard=0, Fine=1, Ultra-Fine=2)

**From the machine decisions:**
- Turning needed? (yes/no)
- Milling needed? (yes/no)
- Drilling needed? (yes/no)
- Grinding needed? (yes/no)

**Calculated combinations:**
- Complexity score (combines holes, pockets, and shape complexity)
- Drilling work (holes times depth - total drilling effort)

---

## Why exactly 17?

Because the AI was trained on exactly 17 inputs. If we give it more or fewer, it won't work. It's like a lock that needs exactly the right key.

Also, the **order must stay the same** every time (the same “slot” must always mean the same feature). This is why we keep a fixed feature list (schema) in code.

One more judge-friendly note: we don’t need to “normalize” these numbers for this model, because Random Forest works by splitting on thresholds (not by measuring distances).

---

## What does this step produce?

A single row of 17 numbers that the AI model reads in Step 8 to predict how long machining will take.
