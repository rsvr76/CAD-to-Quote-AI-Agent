# Step 0 - The Brain of the System (Grandma Version)

---

## What are we actually doing here?

Before any measuring, calculating, or predicting happens, someone needs to be in charge. That's what the AI Agent is - the brain that coordinates everything.

Think of it like a project manager at a construction site. The project manager doesn't lay bricks, pour concrete, or wire electricity - but they decide WHO does WHAT and WHEN. Without the project manager, workers would bump into each other and nothing would get done in the right order.

---

## What does the AI Agent do?

It follows a simple plan:

1. **Understand** - "The engineer wants a cost quote for this metal part"
2. **Gather info** - "I need to know: how many holes? what material? how precise?"
3. **Call the right tools** - "First measure the shape, then figure out which processes are needed and show machine options, then predict the time, then calculate costs"
4. **Explain** - "Here's your quote, and here's WHY it costs this much"
5. **Suggest improvements** - "If you change this one thing, you could save ₹2,400"

This is useful because manufacturing quoting isn’t one calculation — it’s a sequence of specialized steps that must be coordinated in the right order.

---

## What tools does the Agent use?

| Tool | What it does | Analogy |
|---|---|---|
| CAD Analyzer | Measures the 3D shape | Like a surveyor measuring a building |
| Process Router | Decides which processes are needed (and shows machine options) | Like a chef picking which kitchen tools to use |
| ML Predictor | Predicts how long machining takes | Like an experienced worker estimating time |
| Cost Engine | Calculates the price | Like an accountant adding up the bill |
| Explainer | Shows why it costs that much | Like a teacher showing their work |
| DFM Advisor | Suggests cheaper design changes | Like a consultant finding savings |

---

## Why call it an "Agent" instead of a "Calculator"?

A calculator just does math: you put in numbers, you get a number out.

An agent is more than a calculator:
- It **asks questions** when it doesn't have enough information
- It **coordinates** a standard tool chain in the right order
- It **explains** its reasoning
- It **suggests improvements** you didn't ask for

That's the difference between a vending machine (calculator) and a helpful shop assistant (agent).

## What does this step produce?

The agent wakes up, understands what the engineer needs, and starts coordinating all the other steps (1 through 13) in the right order.
